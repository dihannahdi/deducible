We want a profit-sharing investment partnership — a mudarabah. One party, the capital
provider (the rabb al-mal), supplies the entire investment capital. The other party, the
entrepreneur (the mudarib), contributes only labour and expertise, no capital at all. An
independent valuer attests the realised value of the venture at the end; the entrepreneur
must not be able to self-report it. Profits are divided by a pre-agreed ratio — sixty
percent to the capital provider and forty percent to the entrepreneur — and never as a
fixed, guaranteed amount. Any financial loss is borne by the capital provider alone; the
entrepreneur loses only his effort, unless an agreed arbiter rules that the entrepreneur
was negligent or in breach. The capital is not guaranteed. The lifecycle is: fund the
capital, report the realised return, then settle.
