# Istiftā' — Questions for a Qualified Shari'ah Board

This document is a **request for a ruling**, not a ruling. We ask qualified scholars of
*fiqh al-mu'āmalāt* to examine the *Musharakah Mutanaqisah* mechanism implemented in this
repository and to rule on the questions below.

**Two essential caveats:**
1. **No fatwa is claimed by this project.** The artifact only renders the contract's conditions
   *auditable*; it cannot declare anything *ḥalāl*.
2. **Any Arabic evidences (*ibārāt*) circulated alongside these questions — including
   AI-assembled compilations — must be verified (*takhrīj*) against primary sources by a
   qualified human before reliance.** Confident, well-formatted text is not authenticity, and
   misattribution to the Prophet ﷺ or to scripture is a grave matter.

Please rule with evidences and madhhab basis, and indicate which matters are *ijmā'/established*
versus *ijtihādī*. A ruling may depend on the real instrument's particulars (a real leasable
asset, real parties, jurisdiction); please note where that is so.

## Layer A — the partnership structure

1. **The undertaking to purchase (*al-wa'd*).** Client buyouts are *voluntary acts*, not a
   single binding bilateral promise. Does this avoid *'aqdān fī 'aqd* and an impermissible
   binding mutual promise? *(Known point of khilāf.)*
2. **No guarantee of capital.** Settlement pays each partner its share of the asset's *current*
   value, so the financier can lose principal. Does the rent-plus-buyout structure remain free
   of any disguised guarantee of the financier's capital (the *ribā* seam; Qur'an 2:275)?
3. **Loss strictly by capital ratio.** Loss is apportioned by live ownership share
   (*al-ghunm bil-ghurm*; *al-kharāj bil-ḍamān*). Is our proportional loss-sharing correct?
4. **Rent (*ijārah*).** Rent is charged only on the financier's *living* share of a real
   co-owned, leasable asset. Is rent-proportional-to-ownership valid, and what must the
   underlying real asset satisfy?
5. **Known price (*thaman ma'lūm*).** Each buyout is priced at the valuation attested at the
   moment of purchase. Does price-determined-at-execution satisfy *thaman ma'lūm*?

## Layer B — novel to the blockchain implementation

6. **Token as ownership (*milk*) & possession (*qabḍ*).** Does holding the ownership token
   constitute real *shar'ī* ownership (with *ḍamān*), and does on-chain transfer constitute
   valid *qabḍ*?
7. **The valuation oracle & *gharar*.** Does reliance on an independent third-party valuer
   introduce prohibited *gharar*, or is an attested independent valuation acceptable — and on
   what conditions?
8. **Code as offer & acceptance (*ījāb wa qabūl*) and consent (*riḍā*).** Is a contract
   concluded by signed transactions a valid *'aqd*, with capacity and genuine consent?
9. **The contract as agent (*wakīl*).** Is it sound to treat the smart contract as a *wakīl*
   executing the parties' instructions within the limits of *wakālah*?
10. **Immutability vs. rescission (*faskh*).** We encode *khiyar al-shart*, *iqālah*,
    *khiyar al-'ayb* (adjudicated by an agreed arbiter), and an authoritative *faskh* path.
    (a) Are these valid encodings? (b) Is the provision for authoritative rescission sufficient
    to avoid foreclosing lawful redress?
11. **The medium (HBAR / network fees).** Is transacting on a public ledger, and paying fees in
    its native token, permissible? *(Touches the broader khilāf on crypto-assets; please state
    your position, as it gates the rest.)*
12. **The loss residue.** On settlement the impaired remainder is directed to an agreed
    *maslahah*/*waqf* fund rather than stranded. Is this an acceptable disposition (avoiding
    *iḍā'at al-māl*), or is another disposition required?
